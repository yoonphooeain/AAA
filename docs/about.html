Section Summary

This section explains why customer satisfaction matters in banking research and what the study is trying to solve.

◌ Research Focus Banking service quality
◆ Core Problem Uneven customer satisfaction
△ Dependent Variable Overall customer satisfaction

Background of the Study

The banking sector is becoming more competitive, and customer satisfaction has become a key indicator of service success. Banks no longer compete only through financial products. They also compete through service experience, trust, convenience, and digital accessibility.

Problem Statement

Although banks provide a range of services, customers may still experience long waiting times, inconsistent staff behavior, weak digital convenience, or security concerns. These issues may reduce satisfaction and weaken customer loyalty.

Objectives of the Study

This study aims to examine the level of customer satisfaction in banking services, identify the factors affecting satisfaction, and provide recommendations for improving banking service quality.

Research Questions

The study asks three main questions: What is the level of customer satisfaction in banking services? Which service factors most strongly affect satisfaction? How can banks improve service quality and increase satisfaction?

Scope of the Study

The website focuses on selected bank customers and examines service quality factors such as staff behavior, waiting time, security, and digital convenience. The main dependent variable is customer satisfaction.
